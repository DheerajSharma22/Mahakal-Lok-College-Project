Saptarishi

The Saptarishi or seven sages of Sanathan Dharma are the mind born sons of Lord Brahma – The creator of universe. This Great Saptarishi’s life period is known as Manvantar (306,720,000 Earth Years) serving as representative of their father Brahma.

Tripurasur Vadh

According to mythology, Lord Shiva killed a mighty demon named Tripurasura on this day. Due to this the gods got freedom from the atrocities of this demon and the gods were happy and named Lord Shiva as Tripurari.

Vishpan

Lord Shiva's throat turned blue after drinking the poison named Kalkut, which came out of the churning of the ocean, due to which he was called Neelkanth. Mahadev had saved this world only by drinking this poison.

Shiv Barat

Everyone was surprised when Shivji brought the procession. Because there were ghosts and spirits in Shankar ji's procession, because Shivji is favored by the gods as well as the demons.

Rudra Sagar

Worshiping Rudrasagar, which is included in the seven seas of the religious city of Ujjain, has special significance. It is believed that the worries of seven births go away just by seeing the Rudrasagar behind the Mahakal temple.

Nandi Dwar

Admission to the corridor is free. There are three entrances to enter the entire corridor, the main one being the Nandi Gate. There are 108 pillars, 192 sculptures and 53 walls in this 900 meter long corridor, which tell stories related to Lord Shiva.
